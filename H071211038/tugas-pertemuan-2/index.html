Welcome to Makassar Website

Makassar is a thriving port city and the provincial capital of South Sulawesi, Indonesia.

About

Makassar is the 5th biggest city in Indonesia and also the capital city of South Sulawesi. This city was previously called Ujung Pandang. Sometimes, local people still use the name “Ujung Pandang” to refer to this city.

Makassar has been undergoing a vast economic development for its strategic location that facing Makassar strait and surrounded by the Flores Sea, make it even more attractive to travelers.

Makassar is blessed with prominent city landmarks including historical site, unexplored beaches, and beautiful coastlines that blend with delicious culinary treats found in the life of a vibrant urban city.

One of the potential sector at Makassar is from tourism, as evidenced by the large number of hotels were built and the tourist supporting facilities such as airport, tourism object, terminal ships and many more.

Tourist Attractions

Fort Rotterdam

Constructed in the 16th century is the only inheritance fort of Kerajaan Gowa-Tallo that still exist and well taken care until 21st century nowadays.

Losari Beach

Losari Beach is one of the most famous beaches in Makassar. Many people come here to enjoy the sunset with their loved ones. The view here is so charming and calming.

Paotere Harbour

A fishing commercial center in Makassar. In this place, visitors are not only presented with a beautiful sunset view but also can enjoy the legendary Phinisi Ship view.

Samalona Island

An island that has a natural beauty with fine white sand and the sea water that is pure blue. There are many coconut trees that give dramatic nuance over the beauty.

Akkarena Beach

Akkarena beach is a stunning black sand beach and is a a great choice for hanging out with family. It offers a variety of means to relax and play for grown-ups and kids.
